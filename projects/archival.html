Archival
–
Installation, Photography

I see memory split into two different categories – factual memories that are easy to recount to others and emotional and experiential memories that are complex and boundless. The latter type of memory is expressed through this bath of water. To understand this experience as a liminal, boundless form, I began placing plotter prints of my experience in Tokyo, Japan inside the bath, allowing the ink layer to separate from the paper. The image becomes extremely delicate and fragile, eventually breaking apart. The process is reflective of this emotional experience that I can’t seem to understand and capture; it is my attempt to archive experiential memories. The impossibility of capturing the ephemeral and the vast becomes physical.
